Four Kinds of Change

    There are 4 kinds of change; Reactive change, Proactive change, Away From change, and Towards change. The 4 types of change are not necessarily positive or negative, and you have some level of control in all 4 types.
    Photo by Nataliya Vaitkevich: https://www.pexels.com/photo/a-close-up-shot-of-letter-dice-6120219/

Change can be positive or negative, and it comes in many forms. Of course, we would like to think that we can have control of the changes in our lives, but that isn’t always easy, and it might even be impossible at times.

Annie and I have noticed that change seems to occur under four different conditions. We have described them here for you. These four types of change are not mutually exclusive, a change in your life can be one or more of the following types; Reactionary Change, Proactive Change, Moving Away From Change, and Moving Towards Change.

Take a moment to think about the different changes that have happened in your life, and what brought them on. Think about the changes you WANT to happen in your life, and what will it take to make those changes happen? What KIND of change will that be?

1. Reactive change

Reactive change is when something outside of you forces you to change. It could be a death in the family, a diagnosis of an illness, or your children moving out. These are changes that you have to make, but don't necessarily have a desire to change.

One of the most important changes in my life was reactionary. You see, my parents divorced when I was 4, and as a result I grew up thinking “I don’t want to repeat the mistakes of my parents, so I won’t have kids”.

This was my wish until one day I learned that I was in fact going to become a father. In that moment, it was like a switch in my head was flipped, and I instantly went from “I don’t want kids” to “I am going to be the very best father that I can be.”

You see, I didn’t have control over the situation that CAUSED the reactionary change, but I did in fact have control over the THE WAY I reacted to it.

    What sort of changes happened in your life that caught you by surprise, and how did you react?

2. Proactive change

Proactive change is when you see something coming and want to change before it happens. If your kids are moving out next year, you might want to make changes now to ensure that you feel good all the time. Proactive change is a change that you want to make, not a must.

Again from the example above, after I decided that I was going to be the best father I could be, I had to take some action. I began to learn about parenting and I started thinking about what would be important for me do as a parent.

I changed some things in my home so that it would be more child-friendly. I purchased a crib and other necessities for the baby.

I made incremental changes to my lifestyle to accomodate having a child in my life. I became more responsible, and I stayed home on weekends more.

All of these changes happened before I became a father. They were changes I needed to make Proactively. These changes were only the beginning, as we will see. But they were important because they allowed me to be better prepared for when my daughter was born.

    What kinds of Proactive Changes have you made in your life? How did that work out?
